About me

I am Aboryo Edwin Angel — an aspiring Data Engineer, Computer Science graduate from the University of Ghana, and someone who has spent years in the field watching data fall through the cracks. I went from monitoring polio vaccinations in Ada West to issuing teacher licence cards across all 16 regions of Ghana, only to realise my life's mission is to build the systems that make critical data reliable, accessible, and impossible to lose.

What makes me unique is simple — I do not just understand data from a screen. I have collected it by hand, in the field, in rural communities, from teachers who deserved better systems than the ones supporting them. That lived experience is the fuel behind everything I am building toward.

I am passionate about education as the most powerful force for sustainable development in Africa. Data Engineering is my answer — by designing robust data pipelines and scalable cloud infrastructure, I intend to transform how educational data is collected, managed, and used across the African continent.

Currently building expertise in Python, SQL, cloud computing, and pipeline development — one early morning study session at a time, before the fieldwork begins at 7:30 AM. Because everything worth doing has hard parts, and I have never been afraid of those.

"I went from collecting data by hand across Ghana to building systems that make that kind of manual work unnecessary — turns out, both jobs are really about making sure nothing falls through the cracks."
